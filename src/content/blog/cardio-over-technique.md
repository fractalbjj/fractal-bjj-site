There's a pattern emerging at the top of no-gi competition. Not a tactical pattern — a structural one. The athletes consistently making finals are the ones whose entire game is built around sustaining a tempo that most opponents can't match.

## What scales with tempo

Some techniques get *better* the longer the round runs. Others degrade.

- Sustained grip pressure
- Continuous frame breaking
- Repeated entry attempts
- Cumulative positional pressure

## The implication for system design

If this pattern holds, the systems that age best in the next 5–10 years will be the ones whose core decision logic favours sustained pressure over explosive finishes.
